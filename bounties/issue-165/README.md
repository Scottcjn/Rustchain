# Bounty #165: Share Why You Starred RustChain

> **Status**: Open
> **Reward**: 3 RTC per valid claim
> **Pool**: 300 RTC (100 slots)
> **Issue**: Share a public post about why you starred RustChain

This bounty documents the claim requirements for issue #165 so contributors can follow a repo-local reference instead of relying on the GitHub issue body alone.

## Overview

To claim this bounty:

1. Star the RustChain repository: <https://github.com/Scottcjn/Rustchain>
2. Publish a short public post about why you starred it
3. Comment on issue #165 with:
   - A link to your post
   - Your RTC wallet address, or ask for one to be created

A sentence or two is enough. The post should be genuine and reflect a real reason you found the project interesting.

## Suggested Talking Points

Examples of valid themes from the issue:

- RustChain rewards vintage hardware mining
- PowerPC G4 systems can earn more than modern PCs
- The project uses Proof of Antiquity instead of Proof of Work
- IBM POWER8 servers and legacy machines can participate in mining

## Claim Rules

- Your social account must be older than 30 days and show prior activity
- The post must be public and still live during review
- One claim is allowed per person
- Multiple posts may earn a shoutout but not extra RTC
- Fake engagement or bought interactions are grounds for rejection

## Community Shoutout

Valid claims are eligible for the project’s monthly contributor roundup. Strong posts may also be amplified by <https://x.com/RustchainPOA>.

## Claim Template

Use this format in the GitHub issue comment:

```md
Post: https://example.com/my-rustchain-post
Wallet: rtc1...
```

If you do not have a wallet yet:

```md
Post: https://example.com/my-rustchain-post
Wallet: Please create one for me
```

## Notes For Reviewers

When reviewing a claim, verify:

1. The linked post is public and accessible
2. The account appears older than 30 days with non-farming activity
3. The content is an original RustChain-related post
4. The user has not already claimed the bounty
