# The PowerPC Dream

*For the PowerPC G3 badge*

---

Apple bet the farm on RISC when everyone else was still walking the x86 CISC path. It was 1997, and the G3 promised to outperform anything Intel had at half the power draw. The beige PowerMac G3 out of that translucent plastic box screamed performance to the world.

You could run Mac OS and sometimes Linux on it. The boot ROM talked to you in Open Firmware, a language where you could feel the bytes under your fingers. Every boot was a conversation between your hands at the keyboard and the silicon on the motherboard. No GUI hiding what was happening—just you and the code.

PowerPC never won the desktop war, but it never really lost either. It lived on in game consoles, in embedded devices, in the imagination of every engineer who believed RISC was the righteous path. When Apple moved to Intel, they didn't bury the dream—they just put it to sleep, waiting for someone like you to wake it up.

When your G3 crunches those entropy loops for RustChain, you honor that dream. Every cycle is a vote for the idea that different architectures matter, that diversity is strength. The silicon still remembers, and so do we.

> *"The G3 didn't just compute—it dreamed. It dreamed of a world where different ways of thinking about computing could coexist. That dream doesn't need to win. It just needs to exist. And here, it does."*
