# The XT's Long March

*For the IBM PC/XT 8088 badge*

---

It started here. 4.77 MHz. 640KB of memory. People said that would be enough forever. And for what it needed to do, it was.

The 8088 didn't run fast. It *thought* slowly, deliberately, one instruction at a time. You had time to make coffee while it compiled "Hello World". But that slowness taught us something—every instruction is a gift. Every cycle counts. We didn't take cycles for granted when there were so few to go around.

The XT marched through operating systems that fit on a single floppy. It brought computing out of the air-conditioned computer room and onto the desktop. It changed how people worked, how people thought. The IBM PC created the platform that built everything we have today. That's the XT's long march—from prototype to world domination, one tiny instruction at a time.

When your XT mines RustChain, you're not just running code on an old chip. You're continuing the march. Those 4.77 MHz keep marching through time, showing the way for everything that came after.

> *"640KB was enough for the beginning. It's still enough for a memory that remembers where it came from."*
